Story January 27, 2026

The Legend: Werner Herzog's Deranged Penguin

In 2007, legendary filmmaker Werner Herzog and cinematographer Peter Zeitlinger traveled to Antarctica's McMurdo Station for the documentary "Encounters at the End of the World."

Near the end of the film, Herzog interviews penguin scientist Dr. David Ainley about penguin psychology and mental illness. What he captured next became one of the most viral moments in documentary history.

"And then there was this one penguin. A deranged penguin. Heading toward the mountains, some 70 kilometers away..."

The scene has become a widespread internet meme, symbolizing isolation, nihilism, and the absurdity of existence. In January 2026, it went viral again when the US Department of Homeland Security tweeted the video.
